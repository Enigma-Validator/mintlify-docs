> **Why regions?** Choose the gateway closest to your users for best latency. **Pricing and quota accounting are the same in EU and JP.**

## Quick limits

- **Rate limit unit** = **credit**.
- **REST:** every call costs at least **1 credit**; paginated responses cost **1 credit per 25 rows**.
- **WebSocket:** **10 server→client events = 1 credit**.
- **Errors** (HTTP status ≥ 400) are **free** — no credits are deducted.
- **EU = JP** pricing & accounting (choose the closest region for latency).

## REST credit consumption

**429 response headers:** `Retry-After`, `X-RateLimit-Limit`, `X-RateLimit-Remaining`, `X-RateLimit-Reset`.

| Request type | Credit cost | Detail |
| --- | --: | --- |
| Standard request | **1** credit | Minimum cost per call |
| Paginated response | **1** credit per 25 rows | `Math.ceil(rowsCount / 25)`, minimum 1 |
| Error response (status ≥ 400) | **0** credit | No charge on errors |

**Monthly quotas (reset every 30 days from plan activation)**

| Plan | Included credits / month | Overage |
| --- | --: | --- |
| Free | 5,000 (renews every 30 days) | Not available (upgrade) |
| Starter | 500,000 | \$60 per additional 100k |
| Growth | 3,000,000 | \$45 per additional 100k |
| Scale | 12,500,000 | \$30 per additional 100k |
| Enterprise | Unlimited (fair use) | No overage (contract) |

## WebSocket credit consumption

| Type | Credit cost | Detail |
| --- | --: | --- |
| Event batch | **1** credit per batch | 1 batch = **10** server→client events (`WS_EVENTS_PER_CREDIT = 10`) |

- **Per-plan caps:** see the **WebSocket** column in the pricing table above.
- **Best practice:** multiplex many **subscriptions** on a few **connections**; use backoff, heartbeats, and batch subscribe/unsubscribe.
