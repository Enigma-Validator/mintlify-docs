## Quick limits

- **Rate limit unit** = **weighted request** (REST).  
- **Heavy queries** cost more (see size multiplier).  
- **WebSocket** events are pooled with REST: **100 events = 1 weighted request**.  
- **EU = JP** pricing & accounting (choose the closest region for latency).

## REST details

**429 response headers:** `Retry-After`, `X-RateLimit-Limit`, `X-RateLimit-Remaining`, `X-RateLimit-Reset`.

**Weights by category**
- **Live (1):** `spotClearinghouseState`, `portfolio`, `frontendOpenOrders`, `userFunding`
- **Medium (2):** `userNonFundingLedgerUpdates`, `webData2`
- **Historical (5→10):** `historicalOrders`  
  ≤ 1 month → **5** · ≤ 3 months → **7** · ≤ 6 months → **9** · > 6 months → **10** (cap)

**Result-size multiplier**  
`request_weight = endpoint_weight × ceil(limit / 1000)`  
Defaults & caps: default `limit` **100** · per-request cap `size_multiplier ≤ 10`.

## WebSocket details

- **Billing:** `100 delivered events = 1 weighted request` (rounded up per minute per connection).
- **Per-plan caps:** see the **WebSocket** column in the pricing table above.
- **Best practice:** multiplex many **subscriptions** on a few **connections**; use backoff, heartbeats, and batch subscribe/unsubscribe.
